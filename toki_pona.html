Grammar summary

Clauses

A, B, and C can be any word except for a pure particle or preposition.
  • A B - A is modified by B
  • A B C... - A is modified by B and C
  • A pi B C - C modifies B, and that modifies A
  • A nanpa B - the Bth A (e.g. mije nanpa won - the first man)

Sentence structure

U, V, W, X, Y, and Z below represent "clauses" as defined above. M and N are full sentences. Q is somebody being addressed, often a name starting with the word "jan".
  • X li Y - X is/does Y
  • mi Y - We/I are/am/do Y
  • sina Y - You are/do Y
  • ona Y - They/he/she are/is/do Y
  • X li Y e Z - X is/does Y to Z - actually Z is the direct object
  • X li Y ali Y e Z? - is/does X Y?
  • X li Y anu seme? - is/does X Y?
  • X li Y e seme? - to whom/what X is/does Y?
  • seme li Y? - who/what is/does Y?
  • X en U li Y e Z - X and U is/does Y to Z
  • X li Y li V e Z - X is/does Y and V to Z
  • X li Y e Z e W - X is/does Y to Z and W
  • N la M - With/If N then M
  • N taso M - N but M
  • taso M - but M
  • o Y - be/do Y!
  • Q o X li Y - dear Q, X is/does Y
  • Q o Y - dear Q, be/do Y!
  • X o Y - we wish X would be Y
"a" can be appended to any clause, and it is also a full sentence on its own.

"lon", "kepeken", "tawa", "tan" can be used instead of "e" in the sentence structures above.
